How to approach a problem logically in physics

How to approach a problem logically

visualize the situation, identify the actual problem, and comprehend the problem

First,deal with the problem qualitative and quantitative aspects of the problem

Qualitative means relating to the nature or standard of something, rather than to its quantity.

Quantitative means relating to, measuring, or measured by the quantity of something rather than its quality

Click to see a visual representation definition of Qualitative and Quantitative

Interpret the problem in light of your own knowledge and experience when approaching a problem. This help to decide what information is important, what information can be ignored and what information may be needed

Solving strategy to use when approoaching a problem in physics

(1) Comprehend the problem.
(2) Represent the problem in formal terms.
(3) Plan a solution
(4) Execute the plan.
(5) Interpret and evaluate the solution.

Specific physics strategy

    How to focus on the problem:

  1. Develop a qualitative description of the problem.

  2. Visualize the events described in the problem using a sketch.

  3. Write down a simple statement of what you want to find out .

  4. Write down the physics ideas which might be useful in the problem and describe the approach you will use .

Describe the physics:

  1. First use qualitative description of the problem to prepare for the quantitative solution.

  2. Simplify the problem situation by describing it with a diagram in terms of simple physical projects and essential physical quantities

  3. Restate the problem and focus on what you want to find by naming specific variables

Plan the solution

  1. Translate the problem into a set of equation that represent the problem mathematicaly

  2. Write down step by step on how you will solve these equations to look at if there's a solution

Execute the plan

  1. Execute the solution that was planned

  2. Combine the equation as planned to first determine an algebraic solution.

Evaluate the answer

  1. Finally, check the work to see if that is properly stated, reasonable, and that the question was answered
